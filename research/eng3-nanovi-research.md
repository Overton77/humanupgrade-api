## CORE TECHNOLOGY EXPLANATION

### How NanoVi® Works

**Mechanism of Action:**
NanoVi® technology uses specific wavelengths of infrared radiation to create "coherent humidity" (CoHu) - water droplets with enhanced physicochemical properties. These coherent water droplets influence the water surrounding proteins in the body, facilitating proper protein folding and function.

**Scientific Basis:**

1. **Protein Folding:** Before proteins can perform their functions, they must fold into precise 3D shapes
2. **Water's Role:** The water surrounding proteins must have specific properties for proper folding
3. **Coherent Domains:** NanoVi creates coherent domains (CDs) in water microdroplets
4. **Improved Environment:** Enhanced water properties support all protein activities without overriding natural processes

**Why Use Humidity vs. Direct Irradiation:**

- IR heats liquid water while microdroplets in air lose heat faster
- Water microdroplets have an exclusion zone water layer on their surface
- Total surface area of droplets is much larger than liquid water
- Droplets absorb IR energy more intensely than liquid water
- Irradiation of microdroplets causes coherent oscillation more effectively

**Primary Benefits:**

- Protein repair and protection
- Reduced oxidative stress
- Enhanced cellular function
- Improved mitochondrial communication
- Accelerated recovery
- Anti-aging effects

## CASE STUDY ENTITIES

### Case Study 1: Protein Repair from Oxidative Damage

**Title:** "Effect of Humid Air Exposed to IR Radiation on Enzyme Activity - Oxidative Stress Protection Study"

**Summary:**
A peer-reviewed study published in the International Journal of Molecular Sciences investigated NanoVi's ability to affect protein activity when exposed to damaging factors including heat, oxidation, and chemical denaturants. The study examined three enzymes (alkaline phosphatase, peroxidase, and catalase) with both protective (pre-treatment) and restorative (post-treatment) protocols.

**Key Findings - Oxidative Damage:**

- Peroxidase samples treated with NanoVi BEFORE damage: 45% activity restored (35% more than damaged control)
- Peroxidase samples treated AFTER damage: 42% activity restored (32% more than damaged control)
- Alkaline phosphatase treated AFTER oxidation: 19.8% activity restored (more than 3x the oxidized control)
- Alkaline phosphatase treated BEFORE oxidation: 18.1% activity saved
- Catalase showed significant benefit only with post-oxidation treatment

**Key Findings - Heat Damage:**

- Peroxidase: 25% average increase before inactivation, 15% increase after (vs. inactivated control)
- Alkaline phosphatase: 30% average increase after heat damage
- Catalase: 18% increase (before) and 13.4% increase (after) compared to damaged control
- NanoVi showed stabilizing/preventative effect on peroxidase and catalase

**Key Findings - Chemical Damage:**

- Peroxidase treated BEFORE denaturation: 53% activity (66% more than denatured control)
- Peroxidase treated AFTER denaturation: 61% activity (89% more than denatured control)
- Alkaline phosphatase treated AFTER unfolding: 36% activity (1.5x the unfolded control)
- Catalase: Significant effect only with post-denaturation treatment

**Methodology:**

- Placebo-controlled with inactivated devices as controls
- Proteins exposed to humid air treated with NanoVi's IR radiation
- Multiple damage types tested: heat, oxidation, chemical denaturants
- Both pre-treatment (protective) and post-treatment (restorative) protocols

**Source Type:** PUBMED / Peer-Reviewed Journal Article

**URL:** https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/35054784/

**Full Article:** https://www.mdpi.com/1422-0067/23/2/601 (International Journal of Molecular Sciences)

**Published:** International Journal of Molecular Sciences (peer-reviewed, highly ranked)

**Clinical Significance:**
"Protein oxidation is a well-known reason for aging. As proteins are responsible for managing a vast number of cell functions, their oxidation leads to a gradual accumulation of faults and cellular failures. Oxidation of a protein leads to the formation of chemical radicals within amino acids themselves and increases the chance of structural deformation."

**Quote from Study:**
